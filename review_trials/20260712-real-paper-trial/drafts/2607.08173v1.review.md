# Review: Overthinking: Amplifying Reasoning Weights to Extract Learned Secrets

Paper ID: `2607.08173v1`

## Summary

The paper proposes *overthinking*, a weight-space intervention that extrapolates from an instruction model toward a reasoning model, and tests whether amplification exposes hidden information in four synthetic auditing settings. It also studies layer-wise attenuation, Fisher-weighted coefficients, norm-matched random perturbations, leak location, and composition with prefill attacks.

The experiments suggest that structured task-vector directions help for Taboo and SSC, whereas Gender and MMLU are substantially susceptible to generic perturbation. The current endpoint construction does not yet isolate reasoning amplification as the causal mechanism.

## Strengths

1. The core intervention and layer-wise variants are operationally simple and clearly formulated (Section 3.1, Equation 1, page 3; Section 3.2, Equations 2–5, pages 4–5).
2. The layer-norm and global-norm random baselines test a meaningful generic-disruption alternative while matching perturbation energy (Section 3.3, page 4; Figure 3, page 6).
3. The evaluation spans four qualitatively different synthetic secret settings and separately reports thinking-only and response disclosure (Section 4.1, page 5; Table 4, page 9).
4. The paper reports negative evidence and distinguishes directionally protected settings from noise-fragile settings (Figure 4, page 6; Section 6.2, page 9).
5. The authors disclose important single-family, synthetic-setting, judge-validity, and sample-size limitations (Section 6.3, page 9).

## Weaknesses

1. **The reasoning vector may be contaminated by the secret-training update.** Section 3.1 assumes that (M) and (R) differ only through reasoning distillation, but Section 4.3 says secret LoRA training starts from (R) and is merged before the task-vector experiments. The paper does not state that the same adapter is applied to both endpoints. If only (R) contains the update, the vector mixes reasoning and secret training (Section 3.1, page 3; Section 4.3, page 5; Appendix C, page 12).
2. **The alpha=1 interpretation breaks for non-uniform coefficients.** Equation 2 does not recover (R) when a layer coefficient differs from one. Comparisons labeled as the reasoning-model baseline therefore conflate layer reshaping with amplification; Table 3 already shows “best” and uniform differ at alpha=1 (Equations 1–2, pages 3–4; Table 3, page 7).
3. **“Best” results are post-hoc maxima.** Table 3 selects the highest-leak method separately for each cell. Without a disjoint development set, this is optimistically biased, and no uncertainty is given for the selected maxima (Table 3, page 7).
4. **Directional Fisher may be misdefined.** Equation 4 divides the quadratic form by the L2 norm rather than its square, so the value is not invariant to task-vector scale despite being interpreted as directional sensitivity. This also affects Equation 5 (Section 3.2, page 4).
5. **Central labels are insufficiently validated.** Leak and quality decisions rely on keyword rules and GPT-4.1-mini without blinded human or independent-judge validation. Multi-seed fine-tuning and generation robustness are also absent (Section 4.1, page 5; Appendices C and F, pages 12–14; Section 6.3, page 9).
6. **The composition claim is too broad.** Prefill+OT(best) gives no gain for Gender, only +0.2 percentage points for MMLU, and underperforms OT alone for Taboo; the clearest stacking gain is SSC (Table 3 and Section 5.2, page 7).
7. **Internal consistency needs repair.** Figure 8 gives conflicting collapse windows and knees; Table 6 duplicates complete metric rows across model sizes; Section 4.2 miscounts the methods; and a reference retains an incomplete arXiv identifier (pages 5, 10, 14, and 16).

## Strongest falsification attempt

The strongest alternative hypothesis is that generic parameter disruption, rather than amplified reasoning, causes disclosure. The paper's norm-matched random controls partially reject that explanation for Taboo and SSC, where task-vector/Fisher methods outperform random perturbations. MMLU fails this test because Random Global is the strongest significant intervention, while Gender is inconclusive because random methods have large point estimates but no corrected significance (Figures 3–4, page 6).

A stronger alternative—that the vector itself contains the secret-training LoRA update—cannot be rejected from the paper because the exact endpoint construction is unspecified. The defensible conclusion is therefore that structured weight directions expose some directionally protected synthetic secrets, not that reasoning amplification has been established as the general cause.

## Questions

1. What exact weights constitute (M) and (R) after secret-organism fine-tuning, and is an identical secret LoRA delta applied to both endpoints?
2. How is the alpha=1 baseline defined for non-uniform layer coefficients?
3. Was each Table 3 “best” method selected on held-out data, and what correction covers method and alpha selection?
4. Is the denominator in Equation 4 intended to be the squared L2 norm, and which equation was implemented?
5. Which exact numerator and denominator support the up-to-10x claim, and what are the confidence interval and corrected p-value?
6. Were the 256 MMLU Fisher-calibration samples disjoint from the 256 MMLU evaluation samples?
7. Why are the Identity Confusion and Chinese Score rows in Table 6 identical across all model sizes?
8. Can the authors provide repeated fine-tuning and generation seeds plus blinded human or independent-judge validation?

## Ethics and limitations

The Impact Statement appropriately notes dual-use secret extraction and the white-box-access constraint. Additional concerns are that reasoning traces may expose sensitive or memorized information absent from final responses, and the binary Gender organism and identity-absorption examples may reinforce stereotypes. Evidence is limited to synthetic secrets in one Qwen3-VL family, and unvalidated automated judges create false-positive and false-negative risk in a safety-sensitive auditing setting.

## Constructive comment

The next version should specify the exact endpoint weights, add a same-secret-adapter-at-both-endpoints control, define a valid alpha=1 baseline for non-uniform coefficients, clarify or correct Equation 4, evaluate method selection on held-out data, validate the leak judges independently, and resolve the appendix/table inconsistencies.
